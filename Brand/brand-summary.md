# Savage by Design — Brand Summary

## Overview

Savage by Design is a performance-first training and lifestyle brand built around the belief that elite outcomes are not accidental — they are engineered. The brand blends strength, conditioning, combat sports, and disciplined systems thinking into programs, tools, and products designed for athletes who train with intent.

At its core, Savage by Design serves serious recreational athletes — especially those balancing high-level goals with real-world constraints (age, work, family). The brand rejects gimmicks and embraces repeatable structure, measurable progress, and hard standards.

---

## Core Pillars

### 1. Training With Intent
Every program is purpose-built:
- **Strength** (powerlifting & barbell fundamentals)
- **Conditioning** (rower-based, engine development, work capacity)
- **Combat sports performance** (especially Brazilian Jiu-Jitsu)
- **Longevity-aware progression** (masters athletes, joint health, sustainability)

No filler. No random workouts. Every session earns its place.

### 2. Systems Over Motivation
Savage by Design emphasizes:
- Structured blocks
- Clear progression models
- Decision-driven training (not vibes)
- Data capture and review

You don't "feel motivated" — you execute the system.

### 3. Built for the Thinking Athlete
The brand attracts athletes who:
- Want to understand why they train a certain way
- Value tracking, review, and iteration
- Prefer frameworks over hype

This philosophy extends into tooling, including the Savage by Design Workout App, which treats training blocks, sessions, and progression as first-class objects — not loose notes.

## Target Audience

### Primary: Serious Recreational Athletes
- Age: Typically 30-50+ (masters athletes)
- Goals: High-level performance while managing real-world constraints
- Training background: Combat sports (BJJ/grappling), strength training, conditioning
- Mindset: Process-oriented, data-driven, systems thinkers
- Life situation: Balancing training with work, family, and other responsibilities

### Psychographic Profile
Athletes who:
- Track their training and review progress
- Want to understand the "why" behind programming
- Value structure over spontaneity
- Prioritize longevity and sustainability
- Reject fitness industry hype and gimmicks
- See training as engineering, not entertainment
